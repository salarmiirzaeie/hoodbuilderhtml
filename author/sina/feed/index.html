FAQ

Is hood important in the kitchen?

Heat and steam can make your kitchen uncomfortably warm and sticky without proper circulation. Installing a hood ventilation system helps capture moisture, preventing damage like cracked walls and ceilings. Neglecting to clean grease and residue can breed mold and bacteria. Stay vigilant, and keep your kitchen clean for a healthier cooking environment.

What is the purpose of an exhaust hood?

A kitchen hood, also called an exhaust hood or range hood, is a device with a mechanical fan suspended above the stove or cooktop. Its purpose is to remove airborne grease, combustion products, fumes, smoke, heat, and steam from the air by effectively evacuating and filtering them.

How effective is the kitchen hood?

As per findings from Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (LBNL), the typical capture efficiency of standard hoods ranges from 30-40% on front burners and can reach as high as 90% on back burners.

How many types of main kitchen hoods are there?

There are different kitchen hood styles to choose from, including under-cabinet, wall-mount canopy, island canopy, hood liners, retractable downdraft, and microwave hood combinations. You can even opt for a custom hood that complements your cabinetry. Consider factors like kitchen layout, cooking habits, and personal taste when making your choice.

What type of hood do I need?

For optimal performance, choose a hood with a CFM rating of 900+ for your gas range. The size of your range determines the required CFM. If your range is against a wall, consider a wall hood, under-cabinet hood, or range hood insert. For a range on a kitchen island, use an island hood.

What are the different types of hoods in the industry?

There are three main types of hoods: Enclosing hoods, Receiving hoods, and Capturing hoods.

What is a professional hood?

Commercial-grade residential hoods, known as professional range hoods, are designed to fit perfectly over even the largest ranges. They effectively eliminate unwanted moisture, odors, smoke, and fine particles, ensuring your kitchen remains fresh and clean.

What is a standard height hood?

When determining the optimal range hood height, consider factors like the type of range hood, cooktop, and kitchen layout. For electric cooktops, install the range hood around 20-24 inches above the heat source. Gas cooktops require a range hood positioned around 24-30 inches above the stove. These considerations ensure effective ventilation and a comfortable cooking environment.

How Does Commercial Kitchen Ventilation Impact Customer Satisfaction?

Commercial kitchen ventilation plays a big role in a restaurant’s indoor air quality, temperature, smoke and odor management, air circulation, noise level, and overall ambiance of the place. Therefore, it directly affects the health and comfort of customers and staff.

First, we are going to discuss factors that contribute to a restaurant’s air quality to help owners discover the source of the problem. Then, we will recommend some effective ways that can improve a restaurant’s indoor atmosphere and air quality.

What Factors Affect Restaurant’s Indoor Air Quality?

For a healthy and comfortable environment in a restaurant, the following factors have the most significant effects.

  • Commercial Kitchen Ventilation Design and Efficiency

Inadequate restaurant ventilation systems or improper CKV design give rise to the problems below.

  • lower ventilation rates,
  • insufficient airflow,
  • poor odor control,
  • heat and humidity,
  • and improper pollutant removal.

All of these would mean poor indoor air quality and ambiance.

  • Exhaust Hoods

Insufficient exhaust hoods and filters cannot capture and remove grease and cooking-related pollutants and contaminants.

  • Layout of the Restaurant

How different components of a restaurant are organized, like restroom placement, seating arrangements, kitchen placement, and design, can have a positive or negative impact on the air circulation, airflow, and distribution of pollutants and odor.

  • Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs)

Volatile Organic Compounds and other indoor pollutants, such as dust, allergens, and mold spores, reduce the air quality. VOCs are released from 

  • cleaning agents and detergents,
  • some chemicals and solvents,
  • some building materials and furnishings, like flooring oil-based finishes, paints, adhesives, and furniture upholstery.

Sometimes, the polluted outdoor air enters the restaurant through the windows, doors, etc. This is especially a problem for places near high-traffic streets, construction sites, and industrial areas.

These pollutants not only compromise the air quality but also may lead to health issues, particularly for sensitive groups.

  • Cooking Equipment Quality

The stoves, ovens, fryers, and other cooking equipment all have an effect on the air quality by the pollutants they release into the air.

Besides, the cooking methods, like open-flame cooking, frying, grilling, etc., are also important. Some cooking methods release more smoke, grease particles, and fumes into the air affecting its quality.

  • Cleaning and Maintenance

If there is no regular plan for proper maintenance and cleaning of the cooking equipment, ventilation hood, filters, and the whole place, contaminants and grease will build up leading to odorous and polluted air.

In addition, the chemicals, like disinfectants and detergents, used to clean the equipment and the place might have strong odors and introduce VOCs into the air.

  • Occupancy Level and Ventilation Rates

Each space has enough capacity for a certain number of people without causing humidity and airlessness. While it is necessary to consider the size of the place, ventilation rates should also be adjusted based on the number of people present in the restaurant. Higher levels of occupancy require higher ventilation rates.

  • Smoking Policy

Obviously, if smoking is allowed in indoor spaces, there is a significant decrease in the air quality due to the release of harmful chemicals and smoke.

To sum up, the quality and arrangement of the equipment, maintenance and cleaning activities, layout and organization of the restaurant, and some other factors determine the quality of the indoor air and ventilation. Restaurant owners should address any issues related to the factors above and take effective measures to enhance the dining experience for the customers.

Effective Ways to Improve the Ventilation and Air Quality in a Restaurant

Here are some strategies to ensure a pleasant dining atmosphere for customers, and a safe workplace for staff.

  • Investing in Quality HVAC Systems

The heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) system is essential in an essential part of any commercial kitchen ventilation. A high-quality HVAC controls the temperature efficiently, provides air filtration and fresh air (makeup air units), and promotes air ventilation and pressure relationships between different areas.

Therefore, an HVAC system improves indoor air quality by reducing pollutants and odors, and managing the temperature and airflow. Besides, maintaining the pressure is necessary in places like commercial kitchens to prevent depressurization which can lead to the accumulation of pollutants and toxic gases, such as carbon monoxide, in the air inside.

So, look out for any damage or malfunction related to the HVAC system and hire HVAC professionals to help you with its upkeep and upgrades.

  • Changing the Layout to Optimize Cross-Ventilation

Proper airflow is vital for ventilation and the layout has a significant impact on it. The doors, windows, and vents should be placed strategically as well as the kitchen and the restrooms.

Cross-ventilation means the fresh air enters the place while the stale air exits. And, in a restaurant remodeling the positions of walls, dining space, kitchen, door, windows, ventilation pathways (placement of vents, grills, and registers), etc., are changed to facilitate natural cross-ventilation and air circulation.

Additionally, during remodeling, owners can opt for incorporating smart ventilation systems that manage the airflow, ventilation rates, and temperature automatically based on parameters such as outdoor air quality, occupancy levels, etc.

  • Installing Well-Designed Exhaust Systems and Ventilation Fans

Placing powerful and high-quality exhaust hoods above cooking equipment ensures the proper removal of fumes, odors, airborne particles, and smoke. The filters and fans should be maintained and cleaned regularly to function effectively.

In addition to exhaust systems, installing ventilation fans strategically in the dining area prevents air stagnation.

Besides, determining the proper size and positioning of the exhaust hoods and ventilation fans requires experience. So, consulting with an expert can go a long way.

  • Utilizing Air Purification Technology

Air purifiers can be integrated into the HVAC systems or placed independently in the dining area to improve indoor air quality by removing pollutants and allergens. These devices include HEPA filters that effectively reduce airborne particles and create a healthier environment.

  • Regular Maintenance and Cleaning

Both the restaurant equipment and the place need proper scheduled cleaning and keeping up. Professional restaurant hood vent cleaning, exhaust hood filter replacement, and maintaining and cleaning ductwork and fans need to be taken seriously.

Staff must be trained on how to use the equipment appropriately. Also, they should keep an eye open for any ventilation-related issues and report them as soon as possible.

In addition, as chemicals and detergents can release contaminants and odors into the air, they should be stored in a specific place away from the kitchen and the dining area. Also, proper ventilation is necessary while using cleaning agents.

  • Monitoring Ventilation Systems

There are smart monitoring systems available nowadays that help restaurant managers assess the air quality and take action whenever needed. These devices can also be used to find out which part of the restaurant needs better ventilation.

In all, various measures can be taken to achieve a healthier and more inviting place. Which one of these strategies your restaurant needs to promote customer satisfaction is a matter that should be discussed with experts for a better outcome.

FAQs about Commercial Kitchen Ventilation System

As ventilation systems are crucial in a restaurant, there are always a lot of questions and concerns about them. Below, we try to answer some of these questions briefly.

What Are the Different Components of a Commercial Kitchen Ventilation System?

The ventilation system in a commercial kitchen should include the following.

  • Exhaust hood,
  • Ductwork,
  • Exhaust fans,
  • Makeup air unit,
  • Grease filters,
  • Fire suppression systems,
  • Air pollution control devices.

Why Is the Size of the Ventilation System Important?

Different components of the ventilation system should have proper sizing to be effective. For example, undersized exhaust hoods do not remove fumes, heat, odors, and pollutants properly. On the other hand, oversized exhaust hoods create excessive airflow which leads to negative pressure in the kitchen and waste of energy.

What Is Ductwork in a Commercial Kitchen Ventilation System?

The network of pipes or channels that transport the air and pollutants captured by the exhaust hoods to the outside is called ductwork. Proper size and design are necessary for appropriate functioning.

What Is a Makeup Air Unit?

Make-up air units are devices, like HVAC systems, that replace the stuffy air with fresh air, regulate temperature and humidity, maintain air and pressure balance, and enhance the operation of the exhaust hoods.

Can I Install a Ventilation System Myself?

The short answer is no. For a kitchen ventilation system to function properly, it should be designed, installed, and maintained appropriately. As ventilation is crucial for health and safety, it should not be taken lightly.
