]]> Range hood cleaning is vital to a clean and healthy kitchen environment. However, many people forget about it in their regular cleaning routines.

A range hood is designed to remove grease, odors, smoke, and other particles generated during cooking.

Over time, all those particles can build up inside the hood and its filters and make it less effective. Moreover, this can potentially increase the risk of fire hazards.

In this article, we will go through the details to understand the significance of efficient range hood cleaning. We will also provide you with some instructions on how to clean and maintain your kitchen hood effectively.

Key Reasons for Regular Range Hood Cleaning

Regularly cleaning the kitchen hood in a commercial kitchen or restaurant is very important for some reasons:

– Fire Safety:

If range hoods, ductwork, and exhaust fans are not cleaned regularly, the buildup of grease and other flammable materials can cause a big dangerous kitchen disaster.

– Air Quality:

When the hood is clogged with grease and other kitchen pollutants, it becomes less effective. This results in poor air quality which makes the working environment uncomfortable both for the staff and customers.

– Health and Sanitation:

A dirty range hood can become a breeding ground for bacteria, mold, and other harmful microorganisms. These can increase the risk of foodborne illnesses. Regular cleaning keeps everything and everywhere clean and safe for cooking.

– Efficiency and Performance:

A clogged hood does not work well and can make cooking a pain. Regular range hood cleaning ensures that everything runs smoothly in the kitchen and that the hood maintains proper ventilation and airflow.

– Compliance with Regulations:

There are rules about how often range hoods need to be cleaned in commercial kitchens. As a business owner, you had better stay on the right side of those rules and avoid getting in trouble by keeping up with regular cleaning.

A Step-by-Step Guide to Effective Range Hood Cleaning

Take your rubber gloves and get your cleaning supplies ready. You will need some cleaning solution( mild dish soap, vinegar, or a specialized range hood cleaner) along with a bucket, hot water, microfiber cloth or sponge, and a scrub brush or toothbrush.

To ensure safety, turn off and unplug the range hood. Then, take out the filters or screens. They are usually under the hood and can be easily removed by hand or with a screwdriver depending on the model.

Fill a bucket with hot water and a few drops of your chosen cleaning solution to make a soapy mix.

​Dip a cloth or a sponge in the soapy water and gently wipe down the exterior surfaces of the range hood, including the buttons and controls, to get rid of any grease or grime buildup.

If there is tough grease or dirt, use a brush or toothbrush with the soapy mix to scrub it off. Be careful not to scratch anything.

Rinse the cloth or sponge in clean water and wipe down all the surfaces again to remove any soap.

Clean the range hood filters separately. If they are metal, soak them in a sink full of hot water and dish soap for about 15 minutes. Then, gently scrub them with a brush or a sponge to get rid of any grease or debris. Rinse them well and let them air dry before reattaching them to their range hood.

Check the vent opening for any blockages or buildup. Use a vacuum cleaner or a long brush to clear out any dust, debris, or cobwebs.

Once everything is clean and dry, put the filters and screens back on the range hood. Plug in and turn on the range hood to make sure it is working properly.

Cleaning Tips for Different Types of Commercial Range Hoods 

1- Stainless steel range hoods: 

Use a mild detergent or stainless steel cleaner to wipe down the hood.  Then, rinse it with warm water. Do not use anything harsh that could scratch it up.

 2- Aluminum range hoods: 

Clean with a mixture of warm water and a bit of mild dish soap. Use a soft cloth or sponge to clean the hood, then rinse it off thoroughly.

3- Copper range hoods:

Make a paste with vinegar and salt, apply it to the hood, let it sit for a bit, then scrub gently with a soft cloth or sponge. Rinse with warm water and dry it off nicely.

 4- Galvanized range hoods: 

Give this type of hood a good wipe down with warm soapy water and a soft cloth or   sponge. Stay away from any tough cleaners that might damage the  finish.

 5- Ventless range hoods: 

Take out the filters and soak them in soapy water to get rid of all that    grease. Clean the outside of the hood with a mild detergent and warm water, then dry it off well.

 6- Ducted range hoods: 

Keep those filters clean and wipe down the inside and outside of the hood with a mild detergent or degreaser. Just follow the instructions from the manufacturer for cleaning and maintenance.

 7- Professional cleaning services:

If you need a deep clean or some maintenance for your commercial kitchen range hood, think about hiring a professional cleaning service that specializes in commercial kitchen equipment. They will make sure your range hood is completely clean and is working right.

Common Mistakes to Avoid During Range Hood Cleaning

  1. Don’t use any rough or strong cleaners on your range hood since they can damage the surface or finish.
  2. To avoid any electrical hazards, always remember to unplug the range hood before you start cleaning.
  3. Clean or replace the filters regularly as they can get clogged up with grease quickly and lower the optimal performance of the kitchen hood.
  4. Too much water can cause problems; so, avoid using excessive water during your range hood cleaning.
  5. If you want to avoid any potential damage, make sure to read and follow the cleaning instructions from the manufacturer.
  6. Don’t forget to clear out the vent opening which can be clogged up with dust, debris, or grease. This can reduce the efficiency of your range hood.
  7. To ensure a good thorough job, always remember to take your time while doing any kind of commercial kitchen cleanings.
  8. Make sure all parts of the range hood are totally dry before putting them back together.

Common Reasons Why HVAC Systems Lose Efficiency

If you are operating a less efficient HVAC system, you will face multiple issues. One of the major ones among them is high energy bills. If you find that the energy consumption of your machines has been going up for the last couple of years, you should definitely consider an HVAC inspection.

Here are some of the most common causes that can lead to a loss of efficiency of commercial HVAC machines.

Clogged Air Filters

The role of an air filter is to filter out dust and air-borne particles. In this way, the filters help the HVAC machine do its job properly. So, you should never underestimate the significance of these air filters.

As the air filters continue to do their job, they collect a lot of dust and dirt over time. Often, they get clogged after some time. Clogged or blocked air filters fail to do their job in a proper manner. As a result of too much dust, the air filters cannot adequately circulate air through the HVAC machines. If the dirty air filters are not cleaned, it becomes a reason for the equipment to lose efficiency. That is why it is crucial to not leave the air filters unchecked for a long time. Instead, you should check these parts and replace them in a timely manner.

Dirty Air Conditioner Coils

The air conditioning unit of an HVAC machine has two coils: the evaporator coil and the condenser coil. The former is located in the indoor unit whereas the latter is located in the outdoor unit. However, these coils work in collaboration with each other in order to deliver performance. The role of the evaporator coil is to absorb the heat from the indoor air of the restaurant kitchen. The condenser coil lets out the captured heat outdoors. As a result, the indoor air in the kitchen remains cool and comfortable. But these coils can gather dust and dirt over time and fail to do the job in a proper manner. If the coils fail to do their job, the heat exchange process gets affected and the entire system can fail working as a result.

Dirty air conditioner coils take away the overall efficiency of the HVAC machine. If you plan to avoid such problems, you should get in touch with an HVAC company such as Hood Builder in Denver, CO, and have technicians to service the equipment and clean the dirty coils to restor efficiency.

Dirty Furnace Burners

The function of furnace burners is to produce heat. For this, the burners first mix fuel with air and then ignite the mixture. If the furnace burners fail to do their job properly, the heating system in your facility will be affected.

Just like filters and coils can get dirty, the furnace burners too can collect dust and dirt over time. Dust-covered burners struggle to burn the fuel or ignite in an efficient manner. Whether or not your burners are functioning correctly can be determined by using some technical methods. During an HVAC inspection or maintenance, the technician will take a closer look at the burners and see if they are clean or not. If the burners are filled with dust and dirt, cleaning will be required. Once the burners have been cleaned and re-installed, efficiency will be restored.

It is not a good idea to clean the furnace burners yourself, as it can be risky. The best option is to seek the help of an HVAC company in or near your area for properly cleaning of these vital components.

Leaking Ductwork

The ducting or the ductwork is an important part of the HVAC machine. The function of these components is to circulate cooled or heated air throughout the restaurant building. Compared to many other elements, these ducts are more prone to wear and tear and damage through use over time. Particularly, the joints along these ducts often get damaged and holes are formed. This is how the components start to leak and affect the efficiency of the entire HVAC system. Even if all the other parts of the HVAC perform well, leaky ducts can take their toll.

If your equipment has become less efficient, leaking ductwork could be one of the reasons. In order to reclaim HVAC system efficiency, the ducts will need to be repaired and sealed by a trained technician. 

Dust Buildup in Motor

HVAC motors play a crucial role of operating the hood fans, move pumps and drive compressors. A heating and ventilation system is run by a number of different types of motors with various starting torque levels. All these motors require proper maintenance on a regular basis. 

Over time, dust and dirt buildup degrades the functioning of the HVAC motors. Normal wear and tear takes a toll on these components. As a result, the efficiency level of the entire system goes down. Routine cleaning and lubrication are required to keep all these motors in excellent condition.
