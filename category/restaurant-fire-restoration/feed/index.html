Different Types of Efficient Fire Suppression Systems

A suppression system is made up of different components such as a fire alarm initiating device, a fire notification device, an alarm monitoring system, an alarm control panel, etc.

Each type of fire system is used in a different situation and location. In general, the types include water sprinklers, clean agents, chemical agents, and carbon dioxide systems.

1. Water-based System:

It is the most common suppression system because of its fair price and availability of water. This system uses pipe networks to send and distribute water to the connected sprinkler bulbs.

Since the fire system must be chosen according to the type of fire, water sprinklers can not be used for gas and oil fires. Moreover, water-sensitive properties may be seriously damaged after activation. Fire sprinklers have four types of wet pipe, dry pipe, pre-action, and deluge.

2. Clean Agent System:

It uses chemical agents or inert gas to suppress fire. Its extinguishing agents are safe enough to be used in crowded places. The life span of this hazard system is quite short.

3. Chemical Agent System:

They are waterless and also electrically non-conductive. There are wet and dry chemical agents. Wet chemical systems are efficiently used in commercial kitchens. The liquid spray reacts with the oils and fats on the burning surface to produce foam. Dry chemical systems use chemical powder to extinguish fire. They actually suffocate the flames.

4. Carbon Dioxide System:

This system can be used in large open areas or even for a single piece of equipment. Carbon dioxide lacks electrical conductivity, odor, and color; so, no need to worry about the cleanup after the activation. This type of system does not even damage delicate items.

The Most Suitable Suppression System for Food Trucks

As talked about before, most snack truck operators have to equip their businesses with efficient fire suppression systems. There are many factors effective in choosing the right one. Let’s check some of these factors:

  • The potential dangers,
  • The location and surroundings of the snack truck,
  • The needs of the business,
  • The existing equipment
  • The balance between the cost and the effectiveness of the system
  • The installation and maintenance requirements

There are two main options for the safety of food trucks: wet chemical agent systems and dry chemical agent systems.

A wet chemical fire system uses wet agents to protect the hood, duct, and all the cooking stuff in the kitchen. It uses a chemical agent to suppress and cool down the fire.

When the fire starts, it is detected automatically and then the chemical is released throughout the hood and on the appliances. The liquid spray reacts with fats and oils and produces foam. This foam prevents fire reignition.

Besides, after the fire hazard, all the surfaces and appliances can be cleaned easily.

A dry chemical system provides a perfect fire attack. It uses dry chemical powder which is pressurized into a large tank. This system can be easily installed in any commercial kitchen. It is cheap and you can simply use it when there are not any water resources available.

An important point to be mentioned is that after each operation you must re-fill the tank. Unlike wet chemicals, the surfaces affected by dry chemicals will not be easy and cheap to clean up.

Get to Know the Labels

Before we get to the effect of a range hood and kitchen stove hood on a fire, first we briefly mention the different ways a fire could erupt. Aside from common sense, there are many sources provided by the NFPA, that can help us identify and prevent danger. 

Understanding the NFPA diamond, also called National Fire Protection Association 704, gives us a lot of edge when dealing with fire and its prevention. The labels give information about the different materials you might have in your kitchen and how dangerous they can be. 

You don’t need to memorize the exact number of National Fire Protection Association Labels, for every single chemical. Labeling the chemicals and the solutions you use in your kitchen is enough. It is just a precaution so you’d know what to keep away from fire. 

What Helps You Prevent Fire?

Let’s start simple. Take a look around the kitchen and check your cookers. Grease only helps to spread and increase the intensity of a fire. What sparks it though? Well, a spark of course! After working for long hours every day, you might get so tangled up in the work that you forget regular safety checks. 

The grease build-up is the nightmare of every chef, restaurant owner, health inspector, and especially the fire marshal. Let’s just say it doesn’t really “help” in preventing a fire hazard. Naturally, neglecting the cleaning puts your kitchen at risk of many incidents, including fire. 

Check the Back!

Don’t limit the regular maintenance and checking to what you see. Try to check all the connections to the gas pipes and make sure they are in top shape since even a small leak can turn into a disaster. A proper ventilation system might help with the smell but it won’t solve the issue. 

Don’t Forget the Electricity 

A fire hazard might be misleading in its name.  It reminds us of gas, oil, or smoke. The reality is that the source of many fires can be traced to bad or old wiring and electrical equipment. This is why you should always put devices and wiring maintenance in your schedule.

Corrosion can be your worst enemy when it comes to the wiring and the equipment. The wires eventually start to heat up when exposed to electricity and might, in the best-case scenario, pop a circuit, or even cause a fire if sensitive material is nearby. 

Make Sure You’re Ready

Although we always try to minimize the chances of an incident from happening, it is always recommended to be prepared for when things get out of hand. Equipping your kitchen with proper fire alarm systems, smoke detectors, sprinklers, and fire suppression systems is a must for every owner. 

How does a kitchen hood help prevent fire?

Before a fire erupts, there are many signs and conditions to be met for a disaster to happen. One of which is poor ventilation of air. The grease particles floating in the air from all the cooking must land somewhere. 

Installation of a stove ventilation hood, in addition to the airflow of the kitchen, helps reduce the build-up of grease in your work area. This results in fewer accidents in your kitchen, however, cleaning the kitchen exhaust hood should also be a top priority to keep the equipment running smoothly. 

The ventilation hood is for many, sufficient for reducing the airborne grease, however, to help reduce the headache of cleaning the venting system and also the costs, some may turn to grease filters. This helps owners save time, money, and resources. 

This filtration of the air and cleaning of the airborne grease particles implicitly reduces the threat of fire by decreasing the build-up. However, you will need to tend to the stove exhaust hood as well, just to keep it clean and running smoothly for years to come.

Does the variety matter?

Simply searching for a kitchen hood shows you hundreds of results for different types, models, shapes, and prices. This variety proves that there must be a difference between the different types of ventilation hoods. 

Most of the time these differences are related to the design that the customer has in mind. It is just a matter of taste and decoration. This of course affects the price but what about efficiency? Which model is best for our kitchen? Let’s look at the two main types of range hoods.

  • Ducted Range Hood

The most common type of kitchen ventilation hood and the one you probably use. Connected to a vent, the air flows through the stainless-steel baffles and filters and exits the building. External ventilation offers more safety in vacuuming out the smoke and dangerous fumes. 

Because of its efficiency, this is usually the top choice of most kitchens and restaurants. There is no need for constant cleaning of the filters and since a commercial kitchen is a busy place, a ducted range hood is simply a better option. 

  • Ductless Range Hood

Offering versatility in placement, the ductless range hood filters and recirculates the air back into the environment. This usually requires more fan power. This is not very efficient when it comes to saving energy and time, since the filters need constant cleaning to work properly.

The presence of carbon monoxide, formaldehyde (a chemical produced by heating oil), chemical cleaners, and other gasses and fumes inside a kitchen; especially a commercial kitchen where there is constant cooking, rules out the option of having a ductless range hood. 

There are always other factors such as the size, the power, the energy usage, etc. Which you need to consider. That is why it is important to look for the help of a professional when installing a kitchen hood.

Storage – A Key Component of Commercial Kitchen Design

Flow:

Main Causes of Restaurant Fires

A commercial kitchen is a place where cooking goes on for hours on end. The process of cooking and preparing a wide variety of foods leads to a buildup of grease and grime in the exhaust hood systems. Unless proper cleanup is conducted on a regular basis by professionals, the grease deposits can become a fire hazard, increasing the risk of a fire breaking out in the kitchen manifolds. Whether it is the vent hood, exhaust fan, or duct, all of these can become fire hazards.

The most common causes of kitchen fires include:

  • Cooking equipment (deep fryers, cooking ranges, grills)
  • Electrical and lighting equipment
  • Smoking materials

One surefire way to prevent kitchen fires is to keep all the cooking equipment clean and grease-free. In fact, the NFPA has laid out clear guidelines which every restaurant owner needs to follow. You can minimize the risk of restaurant fire by adhering to the NFPA fire safety codes. The code requires you to keep the cooking equipment and the exhaust hood system clean.
